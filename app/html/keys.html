• Contacts
  • - Assign a PGP Public Key to each of your contacts, which will be used in the case that you want to send them an encrypted email. - You can only send encypted emails to the people who are defined in your contacts list. - When you add a person to your contacts, it means that you are confident of that person's identity, and that the Public Key really belongs to them.


  • Personal Key Pair
  •   or  
  • - To import or create a PGP Keypair, you will need a passphrase, which will be used to encrypt your private key. If you forget your passphrase, your Keypair will be rendered useless.
  • - If you want to receive an encypted email from someone, they need to have your Public Key in order for them to be able to encypt the email.
  • - Your Private Key will be used to decrypt a message that was sent to you and was encrypted with your Public Key mentioned above, as well as to sign the emails that you send.
