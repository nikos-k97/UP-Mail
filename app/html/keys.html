• Contacts
  • - Assign a PGP Public Key to each of your contacts, which will be used in the case that you want to send them an encrypted email, as well as to verify their PGP signature. - PGP encryption/ signing features can only be used when the sender or recepient of your emails is a person who is defined in your contacts list. - When you add a person to your contacts, it means that you are confident of that person's identity, and that the Public Key really belongs to them.


  • Personal Key Pair
  •   or  
  • - To import or create a PGP Keypair, you will need a passphrase, which will be used to encrypt your private key. If you forget your passphrase, your Keypair will be rendered useless.
  • - If you choose to Export your PGP Keypair to use it on another email client, you should remember your private key passphrase, since the private key is exported in encrypted form.
  • - If you want to receive an encypted email from someone, they need to have your Public Key in order for them to be able to encypt the email.
  • - Your Private Key will be used to decrypt a message that was sent to you and was encrypted with your Public Key mentioned above, as well as to sign the emails that you send.
